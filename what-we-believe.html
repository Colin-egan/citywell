Theology

What We Believe

Our convictions are rooted in the historic Christian faith, held with humility and open hands.

We hold these beliefs not as a checklist for membership but as the living convictions that shape how we read Scripture, practice community, and engage the world. We encourage questions — doubts don't disqualify you from belonging here.

01

God

We believe in one God who exists eternally as Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — three persons, one essence. God is the creator of all things, the sustainer of life, and the source of all love and beauty.

02

Scripture

We believe the Bible is the inspired Word of God — authoritative, sufficient, and alive. We read it not as a rulebook but as the story of God's relentless love for humanity, culminating in Jesus.

03

Jesus

We believe Jesus of Nazareth is fully God and fully human — born of a virgin, crucified for our sin, raised from the dead, and ascended to the Father. He is Lord of all and our only hope of salvation.

04

The Holy Spirit

We believe the Holy Spirit is the active presence of God in the world today — convicting, comforting, guiding, and empowering the church for its mission.

05

Humanity & Sin

We believe every person is made in the image of God and possesses inherent dignity and worth. We also believe that all people fall short of God's glory and are in need of grace and redemption.

06

Salvation

We believe salvation is a gift of grace received through faith in Jesus — not earned by good works or religious performance. We are saved to belong to God and to one another.

07

The Church

We believe the church is the called-out community of those who follow Jesus — imperfect, but commissioned to embody and announce the Kingdom of God in every neighborhood and generation.

08

Resurrection & New Creation

We believe in the bodily resurrection of Jesus and, in him, the resurrection of all people. We live in expectation of the renewal of all things — a new heaven and a new earth where justice, shalom, and love reign forever.

For all who thirst.
